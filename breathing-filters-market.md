<p><p>Breathing filters include mechanical filters and electrostatic filters. Mechanical filters work by physically blocking particles from passing through the filter, while electrostatic filters attract and trap particles using an electrical charge. Both types of filters are used in respiratory devices such as ventilators and CPAP machines to remove impurities from the air before it is inhaled. The market for breathing filters is growing due to the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases and the need for cleaner air in medical settings.</p></p>
