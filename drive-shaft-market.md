<p><p>There are two main types of drive shafts in the market: hollow and rigid. Hollow drive shafts are designed with a hollow core to reduce the overall weight of the shaft and improve fuel efficiency. They are commonly used in vehicles where weight savings are a priority. On the other hand, rigid drive shafts are solid and provide more strength and durability. They are often used in heavy-duty vehicles and industrial machinery where high torque and power transmission are required.</p></p>
