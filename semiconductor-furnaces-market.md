<p><p>Semiconductor furnaces can be classified into two main types based on their design and operation: vertical and horizontal markets. Vertical furnaces are designed for batch processing of wafers in a vertical orientation, allowing for high throughput and uniform temperature distribution. On the other hand, horizontal furnaces are typically used for single-wafer processing, offering more flexibility in terms of processing parameters and enabling better control over the heating process. Each type of furnace caters to specific market demands and requirements in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
