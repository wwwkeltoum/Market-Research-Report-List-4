<p><p>Polymer-Dispersed Liquid Crystals (PDLCs) are versatile materials that combine polymer and liquid crystal technologies, enabling applications in displays and smart windows. The market comprises three main types: Scattering PDLCs, which scatter light when off and become transparent when electrical voltage is applied; Nano-PDLCs, featuring smaller liquid crystal droplets for improved optical clarity; and Polymer Network Liquid Crystals, which possess a crosslinked network that retains liquid crystal properties, offering enhanced stability and response times. Each type serves unique technological needs.</p></p>
