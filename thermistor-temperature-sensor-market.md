<p><p>Thermistor temperature sensors can be categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact sensors. Contact thermistor temperature sensors require physical contact with the object or environment being measured to accurately determine temperature. On the other hand, non-contact thermistor temperature sensors can measure temperature from a distance without needing direct contact. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages depending on the specific application, such as accuracy, response time, and ease of use.</p></p>
