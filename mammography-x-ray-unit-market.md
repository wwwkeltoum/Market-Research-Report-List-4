<p><p>The mammography X-ray unit market is primarily divided into two types: analog and digital systems. Analog mammography employs traditional film-based techniques, producing images that must be developed in a dark room, resulting in longer processing times and less flexibility in image manipulation. In contrast, digital mammography uses electronic detectors to capture images, offering quicker processing, enhanced image quality, and easier storage and retrieval. Digital systems are increasingly favored for their advanced diagnostic capabilities and improved patient experience.</p></p>
