<p><strong>The Optical Emission Spectroscopy Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Arc/Spark Optical Emission Spectroscopy</li><li>Inductively Coupled Plasma Optical Emission Spectroscopy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Optical Emission Spectroscopy is used for analyzing the elemental composition of materials. Arc/Spark Optical Emission Spectroscopy involves generating a spark or arc to excite the atoms in a sample, producing a unique emission spectrum for each element. On the other hand, Inductively Coupled Plasma Optical Emission Spectroscopy uses a high-temperature plasma to atomize and excite the sample, providing more precise and sensitive measurements. Both techniques are widely used in various industries such as metal analysis, environmental monitoring, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
