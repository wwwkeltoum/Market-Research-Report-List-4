<p><p>Cytomegalovirus (CMV) vaccine market consists of two main types: attenuated vaccines and subunit vaccines. Attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of the virus, which prompts the body to produce an immune response without causing illness. Subunit vaccines contain specific proteins or pieces of the virus that trigger an immune response. Both types aim to provide protection against CMV infection. These vaccines are being developed to prevent congenital CMV infection in newborns and to protect vulnerable populations such as pregnant women and immunocompromised individuals.</p></p>
