<p><ul><li>Resistive Touchscreen</li><li>Capacitive Touchscreen</li><li>Surface Acoustic Wave Touchscreen</li><li>Infrared Touchscreen</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The laptop touchscreen market comprises several key types: resistive touchscreens, which detect touch through pressure and are often used in budget devices; capacitive touchscreens, utilizing electrical conductivity for a more responsive experience, commonly found in premium models; surface acoustic wave touchscreens, which use ultrasonic waves for touch detection, offering high clarity and durability; and infrared touchscreens, employing infrared sensors for touch recognition, ideal for large displays. Each type caters to different user needs, balancing cost, responsiveness, and durability.</p></p>
