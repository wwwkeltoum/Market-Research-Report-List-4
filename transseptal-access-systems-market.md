<p><p>Transseptal Access Systems are utilized in cardiac procedures to access the left side of the heart through the interatrial septum. There are two types of Transseptal Access Systems: Integrated Transseptal Devices, which include both the introducer sheath and needle in a single device, and Non-integrated Transseptal Devices, where the introducer sheath and needle are separate components. Integrated devices offer convenience and efficiency, while non-integrated devices allow for more customization and flexibility in procedures. Both types play a crucial role in minimally invasive cardiac interventions.</p></p>
